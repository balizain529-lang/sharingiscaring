## EDIT 4: MIDDLE SECTION — ON-SCREEN TEXT/ANIMATION (AI Avatar Fix)

**Problem:** The AI consistency explanation (assembled from 3 clean takes) has audible jump cuts. Zain sounds robotic at the splice points.

**Fix:** At each splice point, trigger an on-screen text callout or zoom punch in Descript. The viewer's eye moves to the text/motion — the cut in the talking head becomes invisible.

> **Why not B-roll overlay?** Descript's picture-in-picture stacks clips and creates a visible overlap/split that draws attention to the edit rather than hiding it. Text callouts and zoom punches are native Descript effects that sit cleanly on top without the overlap problem.

---

### Option A: Text Callouts (Underlord) — Recommended

At each splice point, a styled text card pops up on screen with the key phrase being spoken. The visual beat masks the audio cut.

**Splice 1 — just before "We normalize that contact data..."**

**Underlord command:**
> "Add a text callout at the word 'normalize' in the line 'We normalize that contact data so the AI receives consistent information every time.' The callout text should read: **Consistent input = Consistent output**. Style it as a bold centered overlay. Hold for 5 seconds."

---

**Splice 2 — just before "It doesn't think or guess..."**

**Underlord command:**
> "Add a text callout at the word 'think' in the line 'it just formats the prompt to strict and exact sections.' The callout text should read: **The AI formats. It doesn't think or guess.** Bold centered overlay. Hold for 4 seconds."

---

**Splice 3 — just before "That really comes down to two things..."**

**Underlord command:**
> "Add a text callout at the phrase 'two things' in the line 'That really comes down to two things.' The callout text should read: **① System prompt  ②  Normalized data input**. Style as a bold centered overlay. Hold for 5 seconds."

---

### Option B: Zoom Punch at Each Splice — Simpler, No Text

If you prefer not to add text, a quick zoom punch (the camera "cuts in" slightly) at each splice reads as an intentional camera move, not a jump cut.

**Underlord command:**
> "At the word 'normalize' in 'We normalize that contact data', apply a zoom punch — scale from 1.0 to 1.08 over 3 frames, then hold."

> "At the word 'think' in 'it just formats the prompt', apply a zoom punch — scale from 1.08 to 1.0 over 3 frames (zoom out), then hold."

> "At the phrase 'two things' in 'That really comes down to two things', apply a zoom punch — scale from 1.0 to 1.06 over 3 frames, then hold."

The alternating in/out zoom at each cut makes it feel like deliberate multi-camera direction.

---

### Option C: Combination (Best result)

- Use **text callouts** at Splices 1 and 3 (the longer sentences — text has time to read)
- Use **zoom punch** at Splice 2 (shorter sentence — text would feel rushed)

---

**Rhythm fix (apply regardless of option chosen):**
> In Descript, place the cursor between the end of Splice 1's sentence and the start of Splice 2's sentence. Insert a 0.4-second silence gap. This creates a natural breath/pause that reads as a speaker collecting their thought, not a hard cut.
